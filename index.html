RESEARCH

My research is focused on the application of Computer-Aided Drug Design in the development of potent inhibitors against different target proteins of Cancer, Alzheimer's disease, and Parkinson's disease. My research is also focused on the identification of non-synonymous single nucleotide polymorphisms present in oncogenes and tumor suppresor genes by utilizing in-silico tools and prediction of conformational changes in the protein due to mutation by employing Molecular Dynamics study.

About Me

I have completed my undergraduate degree in Biotechnology and Genetic Engineering at Noakhali Science and Technology University. Currently, I am doing my Master’s degree here in the same discipline and expecting my graduation in December 2020. I've achieved National Science & Technology Fellowship (2019-20) as a grant for master's thesis. I am working as a junior research collaborator under the supervision of S. M. Zahid Hosen, senior scientific officer, Molecular Modeling Drug-Design and Discovery laboratories, Bangladesh Council of Scientific and Industrial Research, Chittagong, Bangladesh from 2017 to the present. I am also working as a junior research collaborator under the supervision of Dr. Md. Asaduzzaman Khan, Scientific Professor, Key Laboratory of Epigenetics and Oncology, The Research Center for Preclinical Medicine at Southwest Medical University, Luzhou, Sichuan, China from 2019 to the present. In my educational journey, I gained skills in both computational biology and wet lab research which include the tools of computer-aided drug designing, basic Python and R programming, PCR, RT-PCR, Western blotting, ELISA, HPLC, and DNA Sequencing.
